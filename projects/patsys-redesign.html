Project 05 · Team UX redesign

Patsy’s Restaurant Website Redesign

Improving mobile usability, menu discovery, reservation access, visual hierarchy, and customer trust.

Project type
Team UX redesign
Team
Jihye Park, Shuyue Ge, Carly Shearer, Kevin Lemus
Focus
Research, responsive UX, information architecture

Overview

This team project reimagined Patsy’s restaurant website around the practical information customers need before visiting or ordering.

Problem

The original website presented usability and functionality gaps. Important actions and information were difficult to find, particularly in a mobile context.

Research

Survey feedback showed broad dissatisfaction with the existing digital experience: 65% of participants reported that they were dissatisfied.

Key Findings

  • Mobile responsiveness and small text made browsing difficult.
  • Navigation and visual hierarchy did not clearly guide customers.
  • Reservations and menu browsing needed clearer access.
  • The outdated presentation weakened confidence and trust.

Design Goals

The redesign focused on making menus, hours, location, and reservations easier to reach while creating a clearer, more trustworthy coastal restaurant identity.

Information Architecture

Content and actions were reorganized around common customer priorities, reducing the effort required to move between restaurant information, menus, and reservation tasks.

Responsive Redesign

The interface was designed to support mobile use with larger type, clearer grouping, stronger action hierarchy, and more direct access to reservations and menus.

Testing and Iteration

The team used the research priorities to guide design iteration. Additional testing documentation is Coming Soon.

Outcome

The resulting design direction presents Patsy’s information and primary actions in a more organized, responsive, and visually cohesive experience. No post-launch performance claims are included.

Reflections

Working as a team highlighted the importance of connecting research evidence to navigation, hierarchy, responsive behavior, and the overall expression of trust.
