Project 04 · Participatory design

Student-Driven AI Policy

Turning student voices into shared, actionable AI guidelines through participatory workshops and zine design.

Role
Student Designer / Researcher
Context
Human-Centered Computing course project
Year
Spring 2025

Overview

This course project explored how student perspectives could help shape practical guidance for using AI in shared learning environments.

Challenge

AI policy can feel distant or overly abstract. The design challenge was to make space for student voices and translate discussion into guidance people could understand and use.

Approach

The project combined participatory workshops, collaborative interpretation, and visual storytelling. The process treated students as contributors to policy rather than only recipients of it.

Participatory Process

Workshop activities supported conversation around shared expectations for AI. Detailed research findings and participant information are Coming Soon.

Zine as a Design Outcome

The recommendations were organized as a zine: a tangible, approachable format that could carry policy ideas without feeling like a conventional institutional document.

Reflections

The project reinforced the value of making policy with the people it affects and of choosing a communication format that supports curiosity and discussion.

Next Steps

Future work could validate the guidance with a broader group of students and explore how the zine might support ongoing campus conversations.
